# Critical Raw Materials and Europe’s Energy Transition

## Overview

This project analyses Europe’s strategic dependency on critical raw materials required for the energy transition.

The study focuses on eight strategic raw materials directly linked to batteries, electric vehicles, renewable energy, electricity grids, hydrogen technologies and industrial value chains:

- Battery-grade lithium
- Cobalt
- Battery-grade nickel
- Battery-grade graphite
- Rare earth elements for permanent magnets
- Copper
- Silicon metal
- Platinum-group metals

The objective is to assess which materials create the highest vulnerability for Europe, not only because of geological scarcity, but also because of the concentration of extraction, refining, processing and supply chains.

## Research question

To what extent does Europe’s energy transition create a new strategic dependency on critical raw materials?

## Methodology

The project uses a vulnerability scoring framework based on five criteria:

1. Importance for the energy transition
2. Concentration of extraction
3. Concentration of processing / refining
4. European dependency
5. Difficulty of substitution

Each material is scored from 1 to 5 for each criterion.

The final score is calculated as:

```text
Score = (Energy transition importance × 5)
      + (Extraction concentration × 4)
      + (Processing concentration × 4)
      + (European dependency × 4)
      + (Substitution difficulty × 3)
